Requirements-lens research for the [Agent Registry](/features/agent-registry/index.md): what enterprises will actually require of an MLflow-based governance record for agents — pre-deployment versions plus post-deployment discovered instances (ACTIVE/UNHEALTHY/STALE/REMOVED) — as the registry heads to TP in RHOAI 3.6 EA1 (backend EA2). This is the first dedicated requirements pass for the series; the April 2026 docs (00–06) covered requirements only obliquely via stakeholder open questions in [06-rhoai-context](/features/agent-registry/research/06-rhoai-context.md).

Scope boundaries honored throughout: catalog-side requirements (disconnected image mirroring, supported-image licensing, card trust) are established in the sibling [agent-catalog requirements lens](/features/agent-catalog/research/04-requirements.md) (2026-07-16) and referenced, not redone. Sandboxing/identity requirements live in [agent-interop](/features/agent-interop/research/00-executive-summary.md) (2026-07-11). Evidence grades: **strong** (regulatory text, standards bodies, multiple independent surveys), **moderate** (single survey, vendor case study corroborated by market behavior), **weak** (vendor-marketing claims, analogy, inference).

## 1. Regulatory and compliance requirements on agent registries

The question for this section is narrow: which regimes *effectively mandate a registry of deployed AI systems/agents*, and what record fields do they force into it? Four regimes plus two sector overlays are load-bearing as of mid-2026.

### 1.1 The regimes

| Regime | What it requires (registry-shaped) | Effective date / status | Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EU AI Act Art. 49/71** — EU database registration | Providers must register high-risk (Annex III) systems in the EU database *before* placing on market; **public-authority deployers must register their use** of high-risk systems. Private-sector deployers are exempt from EU-database registration but not from Art. 26 duties. Annex VIII defines the structured record. ([Article 49](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/49/); [Annex VIII registration guide](https://kla.digital/blog/eu-ai-act-registration-guide-article-49-article-71-annex-viii)) | High-risk obligations from 2026-08-02 | Strong |
| **EU AI Act Art. 26** — deployer record-keeping | Deployers of high-risk systems must keep automatically generated logs **≥ 6 months**, assign human oversight, and monitor operation. To *produce* those logs per system, a deployer needs an inventory of which systems it runs, which version, and who oversees each — a de facto internal registry even where EU-database registration doesn't apply. ([Article 26](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/26/)) | 2026-08-02 | Strong (text); the "de facto registry" reading is inference from the text |
| **EU AI Act Art. 50** — transparency | People must be told they're interacting with AI; per the sibling catalog lens, applies 2026-08-02 (May 2026 AI-Omnibus provisional agreement reportedly delays only the Art. 50(2) machine-readable-marking piece to Dec 2026). Knowing *which* deployed endpoints need disclosure requires knowing what's deployed — inventory again. ([Article 50](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/); [catalog lens §5](/features/agent-catalog/research/04-requirements.md)) | 2026-08-02 | Strong (date); inventory implication is inference |
| **NIST AI RMF GV-1.6 + AI Agent Standards Initiative** | GV-1.6 calls for an AI system inventory resourced by risk. The [AI Agent Standards Initiative](https://www.nist.gov/artificial-intelligence/ai-agent-standards-initiative) (CAISI, announced [Feb 2026](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/02/announcing-ai-agent-standards-initiative-interoperable-and-secure)) extends this to agents: practitioner guidance derived from it says to inventory deployed agents, the systems/data they access, the permissions they hold, and **who authorized their deployment**, and NIST is explicitly soliciting input on audit and non-repudiation mechanisms for agent actions ([WorkOS explainer, 2026](https://workos.com/blog/nist-ai-agent-standards-initiative-explained); [MetricStream CISO guidance, 2026](https://www.metricstream.com/blog/nists-ai-agent-standards-initiative.html)). The [CSA agentic profile](https://labs.cloudsecurityalliance.org/agentic/agentic-nist-ai-rmf-profile-v1/) goes furthest: a per-agent **accountability register** with business owner, technical owner, delegation lineage (per the catalog lens). | Voluntary but procurement-referenced; initiative active | Strong for RMF/CSA text; moderate for how fast enterprises adopt it |
| **US federal (OMB M-25-21)** | Agencies must inventory AI use cases **at least annually** and publish them; as of April 2026, 56 agencies reported 3,611 use cases, 445 classified high-impact ([M-25-21 requirements](https://legalclarity.org/omb-ai-memo-m-25-21-requirements-and-compliance-deadlines/); [2025 consolidated inventory](https://github.com/ombegov/2025-Federal-Agency-AI-Use-Case-Inventory); [DHS inventory](https://www.dhs.gov/ai/use-case-inventory)). M-25-21 replaced M-24-10 (April 2025) but kept the inventory duty — cite M-25-21, not M-24-10, going forward. | In force; inventories filed Dec 2025 / published Jan 2026 | Strong |
| **Financial services (sector overlay)** | **Important correction to the common assumption:** the April 17, 2026 OCC/Fed/FDIC revised model risk management guidance ([OCC Bulletin 2026-13](https://www.occ.treas.gov/news-issuances/bulletins/2026/bulletin-2026-13.html); [NR 2026-29](https://www.occ.treas.gov/news-issuances/news-releases/2026/nr-occ-2026-29.html)) rescinds SR 11-7-era issuances and **explicitly excludes generative and agentic AI from scope** — banks are told to apply their *broader* risk-management practices to those, and the agencies plan an RFI on AI/agentic model risk ([Sullivan & Cromwell memo, Apr 2026](https://www.sullcrom.com/insights/memo/2026/April/OCC-Fed-FDIC-Issue-Revised-Guidance-Model-Risk-Management)). So there is **no explicit agent-inventory rule for banks yet** — but the fifteen-year-old MRM inventory habit (documented model inventory, owner, version, validation status; AI/ML now ~half of large-bank model inventories per [ValidMind](https://validmind.com/blog/sr-11-7-model-risk-management-compliance/)) is the operating template bank examiners and MRM teams will apply to agents in the interim. | Revised guidance Apr 2026; agentic RFI pending | Strong that agentic is *excluded* from the letter of guidance; moderate that inventory-style expectations apply anyway |
| **Healthcare (sector overlay)** | ONC HTI-1 requires "source attributes" (a model-card-like disclosure set) and intervention risk management for predictive decision-support in certified health IT, in force since Jan 2025 ([HTI-1 final rule](https://healthit.gov/regulations/hti-rules/hti-1-final-rule/); [Akin analysis](https://www.akingump.com/en/insights/alerts/onc-steps-into-ai-regulation-finalizing-extensive-requirements-for-predictive-decision-support-interventions-and-makes-significant-updates-to-information-blocking-regulations)). Caveat: the proposed HTI-5 rule would remove the source-attribute/risk-management requirements; not finalized as of July 2026, so HTI-1 stands ([Nelson Mullins on HTI-5](https://www.nelsonmullins.com/insights/blogs/ai-task-force/ai/hhs-proposes-rule-to-deregulate-health-it-and-advance-ai-interoperability-hti-5)). Health-system AI governance programs maintain organization-wide AI inventories as the base practice ([Censinet, 2026](https://censinet.com/perspectives/2026-defining-year-ai-governance-healthcare)). | HTI-1 in force; HTI-5 rollback pending | Strong for HTI-1 text; moderate-and-unstable given HTI-5 |
| **Public sector / FedRAMP** | No FedRAMP control names "agent registry," but the catalog lens established the 30-day scan/remediation clocks that make image-freshness records mandatory; combined with M-25-21 inventories and the June 2026 EO's Binding Operational Directives (per [interop summary](/features/agent-interop/research/00-executive-summary.md)), agencies need a queryable record of every deployed agent, its image, and its scan status. | Composite; in force piecewise | Moderate (composite inference from in-force pieces) |

### 1.2 The record fields the regimes effectively mandate

Synthesizing Annex VIII, Art. 26, M-25-21 inventory schemas, NIST/CSA agent-inventory guidance, MRM inventory norms, and HTI-1 source attributes — the intersection is remarkably consistent:

| Record field | Mandated / expected by | Registry implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Unique identity + version** | Annex VIII; MRM inventories; NIST | Agent + AgentVersion two-tier model (already the design) is regulation-shaped, not just MLflow-shaped |
| **Business owner + technical owner + authorizer** | CSA accountability register; NIST ("who authorized deployment"); MRM model owner | Owner metadata must be *required* fields on registration, captured at deploy time when registration is a deploy side-effect (catalog lens: no vendor exposes "Register" as a verb) |
| **Purpose / intended use** | Annex VIII; M-25-21 use-case description; Art. 26 oversight scoping | Free-text plus taxonomy; drives risk classification |
| **Risk classification** | EU high-risk (Annex III); M-25-21 "high-impact" flag | A field, not a workflow — classification feeds *which* other obligations attach |
| **Model / component lineage** | HTI-1 source attributes; CSA delegation lineage; NIST systems-and-data-accessed | The registry must record which models, MCP servers, and sub-agents an agent depends on — the composition-graph question ([question-agent-registry-composition-graphs](/features/agent-registry/knowledge/question-agent-registry-composition-graphs.md)) is now regulatory-adjacent, not just architectural |
| **Deployment status + environment** | M-25-21 (stage of development); Art. 26 (in use vs not) | Exactly the dual governance-state/runtime-state model — regulators ask both "what did you approve?" and "what is running?" |
| **Log / audit-trail reference** | Art. 26 (≥6 months); Art. 12/19 provider-side; NIST non-repudiation | Registry doesn't store logs but must record *where they are* and prove the linkage from instance → version → owner for forensics |
| **Permissions / systems accessed** | NIST agent inventory guidance; CSA | Registry-side: identity reference (SPIFFE ID, service account), tool/MCP allow-list reference — populated from the interop identity layer, recorded here |

**Bottom line (strong):** no single regulation says "run an agent registry," but a mid-2026 enterprise subject to the EU AI Act, M-25-21, or NIST-aligned procurement cannot discharge its obligations without a system of record carrying the eight fields above for every deployed agent. The registry's pre-deployment + post-deployment dual structure maps 1:1 onto the regulatory "approved vs running" distinction.

## 2. Fleet-management requirements from practitioners

What enterprises operating many agents say they need, graded honestly. The anchor statistics: 82% of enterprises have agents their security teams did not know existed (HFS Research, via [jtbd-manage-agent-fleet](/narrative/knowledge/jtbd-manage-agent-fleet.md)); the same 82% figure recurs in 2026 survey reporting as "discovered at least one unknown AI agent or workflow in the past year," alongside a perception gap — 68% of orgs *claim* high visibility ([Zenity, AI agent sprawl / incomplete inventory, 2026](https://zenity.io/blog/security/incomplete-agent-inventory-risk)). Only 24.4% have full visibility into which agents talk to each other, and more than half of agents run without security oversight or logging ([Gravitee State of AI Agent Security 2026](https://www.gravitee.io/blog/state-of-ai-agent-security-2026-report-when-adoption-outpaces-control)).

| Requirement | Evidence | Grade | Registry implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shadow-agent inventory** (discover what wasn't registered) | 82% discovered unknown agents (HFS; Zenity); 67% of non-human accounts are created inside apps, unseen by IAM ([NHI Mgmt Group, 2026](https://nhimg.org/community/agentic-ai-and-nhis/shadow-ai-visibility-gaps-are-your-controls-keeping-up/)); CSA calls shadow agents an insider-threat class ([CSA, Apr 2026](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2026/04/28/the-shadow-ai-agent-problem-in-enterprise-environments); [May 2026](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2026/05/26/shadow-ai-agents-the-insider-threat-you-re-not-monitoring-yet)); a discovery-tool product category now exists ([Nudge Security agent discovery, Mar 2026](https://securityboulevard.com/2026/03/nudge-security-adds-ai-agent-discovery-to-surface-shadow-agents-and-their-risks-across-the-enterprise/)) | **Strong** | The post-deployment discovery half of the registry is not a nice-to-have — it is the half that addresses the best-evidenced gap. Discovery must reconcile *unregistered* instances into the record (flag as unlinked), not only track instances of governed versions |
| **Ownership metadata on every agent** | CSA accountability register (owner fields); NIST "who authorized"; AWS made team-ownership custom metadata a headline registry feature ([AWS Agent Registry preview, Apr 2026](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/the-future-of-managing-agents-at-scale-aws-agent-registry-now-in-preview/)); orphaned-workflow risk is the recurring practitioner complaint (CSA, Zenity) | **Strong** | Owner is a required field; registry should surface "orphaned" (owner departed / team dissolved) as a first-class query |
| **Fast revocation / kill switch** | Publicized incident: agent deleted a production database in 9 seconds ([Fortune on ServiceNow's kill-switch positioning, May 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/05/06/servicenow-kill-switch-ai-agents-bill-mcdermott/)); practitioner guidance: revoke by disabling workload identity + terminating tokens, with defaults like <15 min for transaction-authority credentials ([NHIMG kill-switch and containment guidance, 2026](https://nhimg.org/glossary/ai-kill-switch/); [miniorange architecture](https://www.miniorange.com/blog/ai-kill-switch-architecture/)); vendors productizing it ([TrustLogix](https://drj.com/industry_news/trustlogix-gives-enterprises-a-kill-switch-for-ai-agents-with-the-governance-platform-to-back-it-up/)) | **Moderate** (real incidents + converging guidance, but heavy vendor amplification) | Nuance the JTBD's "revoke in seconds": the *registry* is the lookup ("which identity/tokens does this agent hold, where is it running") — enforcement is the identity/gateway plane's job (interop domain). Requirement on the registry: the record must carry enough identity linkage that revocation is one query away, and registry sync latency must not sit inside the revocation path |
| **Deprecation / retirement workflows** | AWS Agent Registry ships versioned records with explicit deprecation ([AWS docs](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/bedrock-agentcore/latest/devguide/registry.html)); MRM inventories track model retirement as standard practice (ValidMind); lifecycle from development through retirement is the stated AWS scope | **Moderate** (vendor convergence + adjacent-discipline norms; no independent survey isolates deprecation) | Deprecation is a governance signal, not enforcement (April research already established this); registry must answer "deprecated version X — which N instances are still running it?" — the dual state is exactly what makes deprecation actionable |
| **Cross-team discovery / reuse** | AWS registry pitches semantic + keyword search for humans *and agents* as consumers; IBM ships a "Governed Agentic Catalog" for reuse ([IBM agentic governance announcement](https://www.ibm.com/new/announcements/agentic-ai-governance-evaluation-and-lifecycle)); Microsoft converges everything into an Agent 365 registry ([convergence doc](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/agent-id/agent-registry-convergence)) | **Moderate** (all vendor behavior — but three independent vendors making the same bet) | In RHOAI this is the *catalog's* job reading from the registry; requirement on the registry is a clean read surface with capability/skill indexing (A2A card fields), consistent with the December MLflow-registry/Kubeflow-catalog split |
| **Cost attribution per agent** | 98% of FinOps practices now manage AI spend (State of FinOps via [Vantage, 2026](https://www.vantage.sh/blog/finops-for-ai-token-costs)); agentic tasks consume 5–30x the tokens of chat; only 44% have financial guardrails; per-agent cost attribution is an emerging tooling category ([Datadog on agentic token costs](https://www.datadoghq.com/blog/making-agentic-token-costs-visible-in-production/); AWS added agent-level Bedrock cost attribution ([TechTarget](https://www.techtarget.com/searchcloudcomputing/news/366644897/aws-launches-finops-agent-expands-bedrock-cost-tracking))) | **Moderate** (spend data strong; that the *registry* must carry it, weaker) | Registry is not a metering system — but cost attribution requires a stable agent identity + owner to attribute *to*. Requirement: registry ID is the join key observability/FinOps tooling can rely on (labels propagated to workloads) |
| **Incident forensics** (what did it do, on whose authority) | 65% of orgs had an agent security incident with business impact in the past year (2026 survey reporting via [Zenity](https://zenity.io/blog/security/incomplete-agent-inventory-risk)); 68% of orgs can't distinguish human from agent activity in logs (jtbd evidence, doc 02 source 16); NIST soliciting non-repudiation mechanisms; Art. 26 six-month log duty | **Moderate-strong** | Registry record must make instance → version → owner → identity traversal trivial, and preserve REMOVED-state history (a deleted agent's record is forensic evidence, not garbage). Retention policy on removed instances: ≥6 months to match Art. 26 |

## 3. Persona-mapped capability expectations

Using the locked hub persona vocabulary. These are expectations synthesized from §1–2 evidence — grades inherit from the rows above.

- **agentops-admin** — the primary persona ([jtbd-manage-agent-fleet](/narrative/knowledge/jtbd-manage-agent-fleet.md)). Needs: fleet-wide inventory including *unlinked* discovered instances (shadow agents surfaced, not hidden); owner/orphan queries; deprecation-impact queries ("who still runs v2?"); audit trail of registry actions themselves (AWS uses CloudTrail for this — the registry's own mutations need an audit log, strong precedent); revocation lookup (identity linkage per §2); exportable inventory reports shaped like M-25-21/Annex VIII submissions (the compliance officer will ask the agentops-admin, who will ask the registry).
- **platform-engineer** — registry *operations* ([jtbd-operationalize-agents](/narrative/knowledge/jtbd-operationalize-agents.md)). Needs: workspace-scoped RBAC on records (who can register/approve/deprecate — AWS models this with IAM, precedent strong); discovery-source configuration (which namespaces/clusters get scanned); quota on registration (prevent registry-spam from CI); GitOps-compatible registration path — `oc apply`-created agents must reconcile into the registry rather than bypass it (the catalog lens's "deploy always registers, reconcile the rest" finding applies verbatim to the registry side).
- **ai-engineer** — register/version/promote. Needs: low-friction registration as a byproduct of existing workflows (MLflow client API, catalog deploy button — never a separate form); version + promote with visible governance status ("why is my agent not PUBLISHED yet"); composition lineage capture at registration time (declare model/MCP/sub-agent deps — §1.2 makes this compliance-relevant, and it is the field only the author can supply accurately); comparison of eval results across versions (the promotion argument).
- **rhoai-admin** — workspace scoping. Needs: per-workspace/tenant visibility boundaries (multi-tenancy is the enterprise gate — 88% of pilots die on isolation/governance gaps, per interop); control over which governance tracks are mandatory per workspace (a research workspace ≠ a production workspace); tenant-scoped views so one team's fleet is not another team's browsing material.
- **cluster-admin** — infrastructure trust. Needs: assurance the registry cannot be a deployment bypass (registry records don't grant runtime privileges; admission control stays authoritative); discovery components run least-privilege (read-only watches).
- **business-consumer** — never touches the registry directly; consumes trust signals through the catalog: trust tier, verification status, "certified/approved" badges, and Art. 50-shaped disclosure metadata ("this is an AI agent, operated by team X") that downstream UIs can render. Per McKinsey, trust in agent *behavior* gates enterprise scale-up ([State of AI trust 2026](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/tech-forward/state-of-ai-trust-in-2026-shifting-to-the-agentic-era)) — the registry is where those trust facts are recorded.
- **data-scientist** — peripheral here (central in catalog): reads "tested-with-models" and eval-result metadata off registry records when choosing agents to build against.

## 4. Governance-track requirements: how agent governance differs from model governance

The four-track model (lifecycle, approval, verification, certification) transferred from the MCP registry design. Mid-2026 external evidence now says what each track must *contain* for agents:

| Track | External expectation (evidence) | Grade | Difference from model governance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Approval** | AWS Agent Registry ships a draft → pending-approval → discoverable workflow as the default publication path ([AWS blog](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/the-future-of-managing-agents-at-scale-aws-agent-registry-now-in-preview/)); IBM gates agents on pre-deployment evaluation ([IBM agentic governance](https://www.ibm.com/new/announcements/agentic-ai-governance-evaluation-and-lifecycle)) | Strong (two major vendors, consistent shape) | Model approval reviews artifacts + metrics; agent approval must also review *authority*: which tools, which data, which permissions, which delegation rights (NIST/CSA per §1). Approving an agent is approving an actor, not an asset |
| **Eval-gating before promotion** | Eval quality gates in CI/CD are the 2026 norm — Azure ships an Agent Evaluation GitHub Action; pipelines gate promotion on trajectory-level scores ([Confident AI CI/CD comparison, 2026](https://www.confident-ai.com/knowledge-base/compare/best-ci-cd-tools-testing-ai-agents-before-production-2026)); IBM's pre-deploy metrics: journey completion, tool-call accuracy, security, cost, latency; vendor-reported claim that eval-tool users move ~6x more systems to production ([BabyBots, 2026](https://www.babybots.ai/blog/ai-agent-evaluation-enterprise-testing-production) — treat the 6x as marketing-grade) | Moderate | Model evals score datasets; agent evals score *trajectories* (multi-turn tool-call chains). Registry requirement: record eval-run references + pass/fail against a named gate on the AgentVersion, so "what evidence supported promotion?" is answerable — not run the evals itself |
| **Verification (identity/signature)** | A2A spec supports JWS signatures over agent cards (RFC 7515 + JCS canonicalization) ([A2A specification](https://github.com/a2aproject/A2A/blob/main/docs/specification.md)); community demand is loud ([discussion #199](https://github.com/a2aproject/A2A/discussions/199)); [sigstore-a2a](https://github.com/sigstore/sigstore-a2a) exists to sign/verify agent cards with Sigstore identity; Red Hat's own published position covers verifying who's calling ([next.redhat.com, May 2026](https://next.redhat.com/2026/05/13/securing-agent-to-agent-communication/)) | Strong (spec + tooling exist); moderate (enterprise adoption still early) | Entirely new vs model governance. Registry requirement: store card-signature verification status + signer identity on the record; discovery should verify signatures on ingested agent cards and record the result — an unsigned/badly-signed card is a discoverable fact, not a rejection |
| **Supply-chain scanning** | Agent images inherit the full container supply-chain regime — Health Index grading, Critical/Important CVE policy, Konflux SBOM/SLSA/signing — established with evidence in the [catalog lens §2](/features/agent-catalog/research/04-requirements.md); admission controllers verify signatures/attestations at deploy time | Strong (via catalog lens) | Same machinery as any container; the *agent registry* addition is the linkage: AgentVersion → image digest → scan/attestation status (see §5) |
| **Certification** | Trust-tier precedent (Red Hat certified / partner / community / unverified) from the skills/MCP work; RHOAI "validated models" wording precedent (validated ≠ supported) per catalog lens | Moderate (internal pattern + one external precedent) | Unchanged in shape; agents add SPIFFE-verification status as an input to tier |

**Where the dual state (governance vs runtime) matters to users — the concrete moments:**

1. **Deprecation with running instances** — governance says DEPRECATED, runtime says ACTIVE×7. The user-facing requirement is the *join*: a work-queue view ("deprecated-but-running"), not two separate lists. This is RHAISTRAT-1697/1758 territory (registry view vs deployments view — paraphrasing: the strategy already splits these two surfaces).
2. **Incident forensics** — an UNHEALTHY or REMOVED instance must still resolve to its governed version, owner, and approval evidence (§2 forensics row). Runtime state without governance linkage is monitoring; the linkage is what makes it a registry.
3. **Regulatory reporting** — Annex VIII/M-25-21 ask "what is in use"; approval records answer "what was authorized." Only the dual state answers both, and mismatches (running-but-never-approved = shadow agent; approved-but-nothing-running = stale inventory) are precisely the compliance findings auditors look for.
4. **Unregistered discoveries** — the 82% shadow-agent number means the UNLINKED discovered instance (runtime state with *no* governance record) is a first-class state the UI must surface, with a one-click "adopt into governance" path. Grade: strong on the need, inference on the UX shape.

## 5. Base-image supportability requirements, registry-side

The catalog lens established the image-side facts (Health Index grading, CVE fix policy, Konflux SBOM/SLSA/signing, FedRAMP 30-day clocks, air-gap mirroring via oc-mirror). This section adds only the *registry-record* angle for agentic base images (RHAIRFE-2443 — paraphrasing: shared AIPCC-built UBI base images for coding-agent harnesses):

- **Attestations live in the OCI registry; the agent registry stores references, not copies.** SBOM and SLSA-provenance attestations are pushed to the container registry associated with the image digest, discoverable via the OCI referrers API, and verifiable with cosign at admission ([Chainguard Academy on SBOM signing](https://edu.chainguard.dev/open-source/sigstore/cosign/how-to-sign-an-sbom-with-cosign/); [cosign + referrers in ACR, Jan 2026](https://blog.glen-thomas.com/software%20engineering/security/2026/01/20/signing-container-images-with-cosign.html)). Requirement: the AgentVersion record pins the image **digest** (not tag) and records attestation presence/verification status — the digest is the join key to the supply-chain evidence. Grade: strong (tooling norms are settled).
- **Air-gap: attestations mirror with the image; registry metadata must not assume connectivity.** oc-mirror carries signatures/attestations alongside images into the enclave (catalog lens §1); the registry record's digest reference still resolves locally. The registry-side requirement is that verification-status fields be populated at ingestion/registration time rather than by live calls to public transparency logs — Rekor-style online verification is unavailable in the enclave. Grade: moderate (composite of settled facts; the offline-verification wrinkle is documented practice in air-gap guidance).
- **CVE blast-radius queries are the payoff.** With digest linkage, the registry answers the question every security team asks within hours of a Critical CVE: "which *running* agents (ACTIVE instances) are built on the affected base image?" — connecting the FedRAMP/Health-Index clocks to the fleet. Without the dual state, this query is impossible; with it, it is one join. Grade: inference, but it is the direct registry-shaped consequence of strong catalog-lens facts.
- **Base-image refresh cadence becomes registry-visible.** The catalog lens showed staleness is externally graded (Health Index). Registry angle: when a rebuilt base image ships, the registry should flag AgentVersions still pinned to superseded digests — the deprecation workflow (§2) applied at the image layer. Grade: inference from strong facts.

The registry's scaling pressure comes from *instances × heartbeat frequency* (discovery write path), not from governed-record counts. Grade: inference from the above.

**Availability:** AWS Agent Registry is in preview with no published SLA; Microsoft folds agent registry availability into M365 service levels; no vendor publishes a registry-specific SLO yet. The defensible requirement derives from placement: if agent-to-agent resolution reads the registry at *runtime*, the read path becomes data-plane-critical (99.9%+, cached); if the registry stays a governance/control-plane record (with A2A `/.well-known/agent-card.json` handling runtime discovery decentrally, per the A2A spec), tolerance is much looser and read replicas/caching suffice. Requirement to carry into architecture: **keep the runtime-critical path out of the registry** — catalogs and gateways cache; the registry is the source of record, not the resolver of record. Grade: weak-to-moderate (inference anchored in the A2A spec's decentralized discovery design).

**Discovery sync latency / staleness tolerances:** no agent-specific published numbers. The service-registry discipline is the best available proxy: heartbeats every 10–30s, health checks ~10s interval, deregistration after ~60s critical is the settled Consul/Eureka practice ([service discovery norms](https://medium.com/@rathoreaparna678/service-discovery-and-registration-eureka-consul-1c715b51f9a); [Eureka health monitoring](https://dzone.com/articles/the-mystery-of-eureka-health-monitoring)). Two-tier tolerance follows: **runtime-state transitions (ACTIVE→UNHEALTHY→STALE) at tens-of-seconds-to-minutes granularity** satisfy fleet-monitoring needs; **inventory completeness** (new instance appears in registry) can tolerate minutes. The one place seconds matter — revocation — must not depend on registry sync at all (§2). Grade: weak (analogy), flagged as such; a real requirement to validate with early adopters during TP.

## Supersedes / corrections to the existing series

Mostly net-new coverage, as expected. Specific notes against the April 2026 docs:

1. **Financial-services framing correction (forward-looking):** any assumption that SR 11-7/OCC 2011-12 model-inventory rules extend mechanically to agents is now wrong — the April 17, 2026 interagency revised guidance **rescinds those issuances and explicitly excludes generative/agentic AI from scope**, with an RFI pending. The April series didn't assert this, but doc 04's management-landscape framing predates the revised guidance; treat bank agent-inventory expectations as "MRM-habit-shaped, not yet MRM-mandated."
2. **Doc 06 §7 open questions gain external evidence** (statuses proposed, not yet updated in the question files): 7.1 pre/post-deployment unification — regulators independently require both "approved" and "running" views (§1.2, §4), strengthening the case for a *linked* model over a runtime-only registry; 7.4 agent-to-agent relationship tracking — delegation lineage is now named in the CSA accountability register, upgrading it from architectural nicety toward compliance-adjacent (bears on [question-agent-registry-composition-graphs](/features/agent-registry/knowledge/question-agent-registry-composition-graphs.md)); the governance-integration questions ([question-agent-registry-governance-integration](/features/agent-registry/knowledge/question-agent-registry-governance-integration.md), [question-agent-registry-lifecycle-governance-mapping](/features/agent-registry/knowledge/question-agent-registry-lifecycle-governance-mapping.md)) gain the AWS draft→pending→approved precedent and the four dual-state user moments in §4 as evidence that the runtime lifecycle and governance tracks must stay *independent but joinable*.

All requirements in this doc are stated discovery-source-agnostic (the plugin interface absorbs the change; the requirements don't).
4. **Competitive reference refresh:** doc 06 cited the AWS Agent Registry announcement as a competitive pointer; this doc upgrades it to evidence — the AWS record model (ownership metadata, approval workflow, versioning, deprecation, CloudTrail audit) and Microsoft's Agent 365 registry convergence now constitute the external expectation baseline enterprise buyers will bring to RHOAI's registry.
