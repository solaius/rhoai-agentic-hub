SkillsBench (Stanford, CMU, Berkeley, Oxford) is the first quality
benchmark for agent skills. Key findings:

- **47,150 public skills scored**: average quality 6.2 out of 12
- **73% carry elevated safety risk**; 7,034 tripped hard safety gates
- **Curated skills**: +16.2 percentage points average pass rate
- **Self-generated skills**: -1.3pp (negligible or negative benefit)
- **Focused skills (2-3 modules)**: +18.6pp
- **Larger bundles**: +5.9pp

The implication is unambiguous: **quality and focus beat catalog size**.
A smaller catalog of 15-20 curated, working skills delivers more value
than hundreds of unverified community entries.

This evidence directly supports the catalog's curated approach (Red
Hat-provided and partner-verified tiers) over a marketplace volume
model (skills.sh's 669K+, where 36% had security flaws per Snyk).
